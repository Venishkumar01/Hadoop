Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam has always been known as the 'People’s President' and he accomplished a feat in the field of science,
 especially missiles and space. He died on 27 July 2015 due to cardiac arrest while delivering a speech in Shillong.
He will always be remembered for his contribution to Science and society. Dr Kalam's life is an
 inspiration to the people of this country. He was from a modest background and made his way up the 11th President of India in 2002. 
Dr Kalam dedicated nearly four decades of his life to DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation).
He is as much remembered as the former President of India as he is remembered for his simplicity. 
He is also fondly remembered for his love and affection for children. The entire nation revered Dr APJ Abdul Kalam and consider his death a personal loss.
Born as Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, he was sworn in as the 11th President of the country in 2002.
 He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His father was the imam of a local mosque and his mother a homemaker. 
He was the youngest among four brothers and one sister in his family.
Throughout his life in several speeches he has always encouraged people to have the best values and show kindness towards fellow human beings.
 Dr Kalam’s advice and anecdotes are widely referred to by every generation due to their relevance. With small stories and life experiences,
 Dr Kalam used to explain complexities of human nature in a simple way. During his lifetime, he has always talked about the importance of family,
 the values of family bonding, and has given various pieces of advice to parents and kids.
